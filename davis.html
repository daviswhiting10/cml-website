Davis Whiting

Offensive Instructor

Davis Whiting training at High Point University.

Bio

Davis Whiting is a lacrosse instructor and former Division I athlete who brings a player-first perspective to every session. Growing up in an area where lacrosse wasn't part of the mainstream, Davis navigated the recruiting process without a roadmap — learning through trial, effort, and the guidance of great mentors. That experience is the foundation of his coaching philosophy: every athlete's path is different, and the right guidance at the right time makes all the difference. Davis played four years of Division I lacrosse and served as team captain his senior year. He has since applied that experience to coaching both teams and individual players, with a focus on helping athletes understand exactly what they need to work on to accelerate their growth and stand out in front of college coaches.

Experience

Individual Approach

  • Davis coaches with the belief that players don't need to come from a lacrosse hotbed to compete at the highest level — they need clarity on what to work on and a plan to get there. Every athlete gets an honest assessment of where they are, where they want to go, and exactly what it takes to close that gap.

Division I Offensive Lacrosse IQ and Stick Work

Having a high lacrosse IQ and exceptional stick work skills are paramount for competing at any level. Lacrosse IQ encompasses tactical understanding, field awareness, and the ability to make quick, strategic decisions — enabling athletes to exploit defensive weaknesses and create scoring opportunities.

Dynamic Team Offense and Individual Development

Team offense in lacrosse demands strategic positioning, effective passing, and seamless communication. Dynamic individual development ensures continuous improvement and the ability to adapt to changing game situations — giving athletes a competitive edge at every level.
